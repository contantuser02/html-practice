HTML Entities Example

HTML entities are special codes that represent reserved characters in HTML. They are used to display characters that have a special meaning in HTML, such as < for the less-than sign (<), > for the greater-than sign (>), and & for the ampersand (&).

Here are some common HTML entities:

  • < - Less-than sign (<)
  • > - Greater-than sign (>)
  • & - Ampersand (&)
  • " - Double quotation mark (")
  • ' - Single quotation mark (')
  •   - Non-breaking space
